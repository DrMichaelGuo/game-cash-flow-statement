What is a Cash Flow Statement?

A cash flow statement tracks the actual movement of cash in and out of a business over a specific period. Unlike profit & loss statements, it focuses solely on cash transactions, helping businesses understand their liquidity position.

üíº

Operating Activities

Cash Generator

The heart of your business operations - day-to-day activities that generate revenue.

Examples:

  • Cash received from customers
  • Payments to suppliers
  • Employee salaries and wages
  • Tax payments
  • Interest payments
Key Insight: Positive operating cash flow indicates a healthy, sustainable business model.

Investing Activities

Growth Focus

Long-term investments that shape your business's future capabilities and growth.

Examples:

  • Purchase of equipment or machinery
  • Property acquisitions
  • Sale of fixed assets
  • Investment in other companies
  • Software and technology purchases
Key Insight: Negative investing cash flow often signals growth and expansion.

Financing Activities

Capital Management

How your business raises money and manages relationships with investors and lenders.

Examples:

  • Bank loans received
  • Share capital raised
  • Dividend payments
  • Loan repayments
  • Share buybacks
Key Insight: Shows how the business funds its operations and growth.
